Peaky blinders

Peaky Blinders is a gritty, atmospheric period piece that follows Tommy Shelby as he navigates the smoky streets of post-WWI Birmingham. It’s a story of cold-blooded ambition, where the "mastermind" vibe comes from Tommy’s ability to stay three steps ahead of the crown, the communists, and rival gangs, all while trying to move his family from the slums to high society. It’s arguably the most "stylized" of the three, leaning heavily on cinematic visuals and a brooding, slow-burn intensity.

Money Heist

Money Heist, on the other hand, trades the historical grit for modern, high-octane adrenaline. It centers on the Professor, a socially awkward genius who treats a multi-billion euro heist like a grand game of chess. While Peaky Blinders focuses on building an empire, Money Heist focuses on the execution of a singular, impossible plan. It’s famous for its "plan-within-a-plan" structure, where every setback faced by the robbers is revealed to be something the Professor anticipated months in advance during their rigorous training.

Prison Break

Prison Break is perhaps the most literal "blueprint" show of the group. It follows Michael Scofield, an engineer who intentionally gets sent to prison to break out his framed brother. Like the Professor, Michael uses superior intelligence to outmaneuver a system, but the stakes feel more claustrophobic because he’s trapped behind bars. If Peaky Blinders is about power and Money Heist is about rebellion, Prison Break is about pure survival and the ingenious use of logic under extreme pressure.
