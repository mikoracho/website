The boys

The Boys is a darkly comedic superhero series that deconstructs the myth of the "superhero" by exposing the corrupt and dangerous nature of those in power. It follows a group of vigilantes who take on the corrupt superheroes of their world, revealing how fame and power can be twisted into something sinister. The show is known for its brutal humor, shocking plot twists, and unflinching critique of celebrity culture.

Stranger Things

Set in the 1980s, Stranger Things follows a group of kids in a small town who encounter supernatural forces and government conspiracies. The show blends horror, science fiction, and coming-of-age themes with a nostalgic 80s aesthetic. It explores friendship, family bonds, and the power of good versus evil in a world where the unknown is both terrifying and fascinating.

The 100

The 100 is a post-apocalyptic science fiction series that follows a group of teenagers who return to Earth after a nuclear war has devastated humanity. The show explores themes of survival, morality, and the struggle to rebuild civilization in a harsh and unforgiving world.
